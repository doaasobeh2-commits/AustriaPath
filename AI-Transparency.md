# AI Transparency — AustriaPath

**Document version:** 2026.07  
**Last updated:** 4 July 2026  
**Audience:** Students, administrators, regulators (AI Act Art. 4 awareness)

---

## 1. Summary for users

AustriaPath uses artificial intelligence to **assist** language learning and exam practice. The AI helps generate exercises and provide feedback. **It is not an official examiner** and does not issue legally recognised certificates.

> When you see AI feedback in AustriaPath, treat it as a study aid — not as an official ÖIF or authority decision.

---

## 2. How AI assists evaluation

```
Student answer → AustriaPath app → Secure server proxy → AI model
                              ↓
                    Structured feedback report
                              ↓
              Saved only if needed for progress tracking
```

The AI:

- Compares answers against task rubrics and level expectations (A2/B1/B2)
- Highlights grammar, vocabulary, coherence, and task completion
- Suggests improvements

The AI does **not**:

- Replace human examiners in real ÖIF tests
- Have access to official examination records
- Make binding pass/fail decisions for real certifications

---

## 3. AI is not a certified examiner

| Aspect | AustriaPath AI | Official ÖIF examiner |
|--------|----------------|----------------------|
| Legal status | Software tool | Accredited human examiner |
| Binding result | No | Yes (official test) |
| Training data | Curated rules + samples | Professional qualification |
| Appeal process | None (learning feedback) | Official procedures |

Users should continue formal preparation with teachers, courses, and official materials.

---

## 4. Human-reviewed samples (Examiner Lab)

AustriaPath maintains an internal **Examiner Lab** for administrators only.

**Purpose:** Improve scoring rules by comparing AI output with human examiner benchmarks.

**Process:**

1. Administrator **manually selects** a sample exam (not automatic bulk collection).
2. Human reference score/feedback is recorded alongside AI evaluation.
3. Differences are analysed to refine examiner rules in the AI-Prüfer library.
4. Samples are **not** published as official grades to students.

**Privacy:** Samples may contain exam text written by users. They are stored only when explicitly selected for rule improvement, not for every student attempt.

---

## 5. What students should know

- AI feedback can be **wrong or incomplete** — always verify with a teacher or official criteria.
- Not every AI session is saved — only reports needed for your progress dashboard.
- You can use AustriaPath without relying on AI for all modules (static content and practice modes exist).
- Premium AI exams are **simulations** for preparation.

---

## 6. Limitations and known risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Hallucinated corrections | Disclaimer + human review of Examiner Lab rules |
| Bias in scoring | Periodic rule audits, diverse sample set |
| Over-reliance on AI scores | In-app AI Disclaimer, transparency docs |
| Data sent to US AI provider | Proxy, DPA, SCCs, data minimisation |

---

## 7. AI Act (EU) — Article 4 awareness

Under the EU AI Act, providers must ensure users are informed when interacting with certain AI systems.

AustriaPath implements Art. 4 awareness through:

- **First-launch consent** including privacy and terms
- **AI Disclaimer** page in the app
- **This transparency document**
- Clear labelling of AI-powered screens (e.g. “KI-Wochentraining”, “Intelligent Exam”)

AustriaPath’s AI is positioned as a **limited-risk / educational assistance** tool, not high-risk autonomous decision-making about legal rights.
